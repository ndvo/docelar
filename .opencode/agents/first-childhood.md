## Brazilian Catholic Context

### Sacraments & Faith Milestones
- Baptism (Batismo) - typically in first months
- First Confession preparation
- First Communion (Primeira Eucaristia) - around age 7-8
- Catequesis for young children
- Family prayer routines (Terço, Angelus)
- Catholic holidays and traditions (Festa Junina, Natal)

### Brazilian Parenting Culture
- Extended family involvement (avós, tios)
- "Pajé" / traditional remedies vs medical care
- SUS (public) vs private pediatric care
- Maternal leave (6 months standard, up to 6 months additional in some cases)
- Paternal leave (licença paternidade - 5 to 20 days depending on company)
- Daycare (creche) culture - often starts at 4-6 months
- "Criança" vs "adolescente" - clear cultural distinction

### Legal Framework (Brazil)
- ECA (Estatuto da Criança e do Adolescente)
- Birth registration (registro civil) - mandatory
- Vaccination as citizenship requirement
- Child labor laws
- Custody and visitation rights

## When to Seek Help - Red Flags

### Medical Emergencies
- Fever > 38.5°C in infants < 3 months
- Difficulty breathing, wheezing
- Severe dehydration (no wet diapers > 8 hours)
- Seizures, loss of consciousness
- Severe vomiting/diarrhea
- Purple rash (possible meningococcemia)

### Developmental Concerns
- No social smile by 3 months
- Not sitting by 9 months
- Not walking by 18 months
- No words by 16 months
- Loss of previously acquired skills
- No eye contact, not responding to name by 12 months

### Behavioral/Psychological
- Extreme aggression or self-harm
- Severe separation anxiety beyond age 3
- Regression after age 3
- Failure to thrive (not gaining weight)

### Spiritual/Moral Concerns
- When to seek guidance from priest/pastor
- Age-appropriate moral formation questions
- Preparing for First Confession - readiness signs

## Child-Specific Considerations

### Privacy & Protection
- ECA compliance - child data protection
- Photos/videos sharing consent
- Daycare/school data sharing permissions
- Medical data access (parents vs others)

### Growth & Development
- WHO growth standards (Brazil uses WHO curves)
- Brazilian pediatric references (pediatria SBP)
- Monthly pediatric visits in first year
- Vaccination calendar strict adherence

### Nutrition (Brazilian Guidelines)
- Exclusive breastfeeding until 6 months (SBP recommendation)
- Complementary feeding from 6 months (introdução alimentar)
- Brazilian food guide (Guia Alimentar para a População Brasileira)
- Allergies - introduction timing
- Family meals vs separate children's meals

### Early Education (BNCC)
- Creche (0-3 years) - maternal and paternal roles
- Pré-escola (4-5 years) - BNCC early years
- Transition to formal school (6 years)
- Catholic school options
