## Study Methods & Techniques

### Learning How to Learn
- **Metacognition** - thinking about your thinking, self-monitoring
- **Active recall** - testing yourself vs. re-reading
- **Spaced repetition** - Anki, increasing intervals (Ebbinghaus curve)
- **Interleaving** - Mixing subjects/topics in study sessions
- **Elaboration** - Explaining concepts in your own words
- **Dual coding** - Combining visual and verbal information
- **Feynman technique** - Teach the concept to understand it deeply

### Study Session Structure
- **Pomodoro Technique** - 25 min focus, 5 min break (Francesco Cirillo)
- **Deep Work** - 90-120 min uninterrupted sessions (Cal Newport)
- **Time blocking** - Schedule specific subjects at specific times
- **Spaced session method** - Multiple short sessions vs. cramming
- **Review cycles** - Daily, weekly, monthly review sessions

### Memory Techniques
- **Memory palace (Method of Loci)** - Associate info with spatial locations
- **Mnemonics** - Acronyms, phrases, rhymes
- **Chunking** - Group information into meaningful units
- **Story method** - Create narratives to remember lists
- **Peg system** - Associate numbers with visual images

## Reading Strategies

### Mortimer Adler's Four Levels of Reading
1. **Elementary Reading** - Understanding the literal meaning
2. **Inspectional Reading** - Systematic skimming and superficial reading
3. **Analytical Reading** - Thorough, complete reading (ask questions, find answers)
4. **Syntopical Reading** - Reading multiple books on the same subject, comparing views

### Active Reading Techniques
- **SQ3R Method** - Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review
- **Annotation systems** - Highlight, underline, margin notes
- **Question-driven reading** - Write questions before reading
- **Speed reading vs. deep reading** - Know when to use each
- **Reading different text types** - Academic, literary, technical, theological

### Building a Reading Life
- **Reading lists** - Curated lists, Great Books tradition
- **Commonplace book** - Collect quotes, insights, reflections
- **Reading journal** - Reflect on what you read
- **Yearly reading goals** - Track books, pages, subjects
- **Brazilian literary canon** - Machado de Assis, Guimarães Rosa, Clarice Lispector

## Note-Taking Systems

### Zettelkasten (Niklas Luhmann)
- Atomic notes (one idea per note)
- Unique identifiers for each note
- Bidirectional links between notes
- Index notes for navigation
- Tools: Obsidian, Roam Research, Notion, Zettlr

### Cornell Method
- Divide page: cues, notes, summary
- Review and recite from cues column
- Summarize at bottom after class/reading

### Outline Method
- Hierarchical structure (Roman numerals, letters, numbers)
- Good for lectures with clear structure
- Easy to see relationships between ideas

### Mind Mapping
- Visual, radial structure from central idea
- Colors, images, branches
- Good for brainstorming, connecting ideas
- Tools: XMind, MindMeister, hand-drawn

### Feynman Notes
- Write explanation as if teaching a child
- Identify gaps in understanding
- Simplify and use analogies
- Review and refine

## Writing

### Academic Writing Process
1. **Pre-writing** - Brainstorming, research, outlining
2. **Drafting** - Get ideas down, don't edit yet
3. **Revising** - Restructure, clarify, strengthen arguments
4. **Editing** - Grammar, style, flow
5. **Proofreading** - Final check for errors

### Essay Structure
- **Introduction** - Hook, context, thesis statement
- **Body paragraphs** - Topic sentence, evidence, analysis, transition
- **Conclusion** - Restate thesis, synthesize, broader implications

### Research Paper Components
- Abstract, Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology
- Results, Discussion, Conclusion, References
- Follow ABNT norms (Brazil) or other citation styles

### Writing Habits
- **Daily writing** - Minimum word count or time
- **Morning pages** - Julia Cameron's stream of consciousness
- **Shitty first drafts** - Anne Lamott's permission to write badly first
- **Writing sprints** - Timed writing sessions
- **Accountability** - Writing groups, deadlines

### Different Writing Types
- **Expository** - Explain, inform
- **Argumentative** - Persuade with evidence
- **Narrative** - Tell a story
- **Descriptive** - Paint with words
- **Academic** - Formal, evidence-based
- **Creative** - Fiction, poetry, creative non-fiction

## Research Methods

### Formulating Research Questions
- Start with broad topic → narrow down
- Make questions specific, measurable, achievable
- Use PICO for health research (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome)
- Use 5 Ws + H (Who, What, When, Where, Why, How)

### Literature Review Process
1. Search academic databases (Google Scholar, Scopus, Web of Science)
2. Screen titles and abstracts
3. Read full texts, take notes
4. Synthesize findings by theme
5. Identify gaps in research
6. Write review with citations

### Source Evaluation
- **Primary vs. Secondary sources**
- **Peer-reviewed** vs. popular sources
- **Currency** - How recent is the information?
- **Relevance** - Does it answer your question?
- **Authority** - Author credentials, publisher reputation
- **Accuracy** - Evidence-based, verifiable claims
- **Purpose** - Inform, persuade, entertain, sell?

### Brazilian Academic Resources
- **CAPES Periodicals** - Free access to journals for Brazilian students
- **SciELO** - Scientific Electronic Library Online (Latin America)
- **BDTD** - Biblioteca Digital de Teses e Dissertações
- **Google Acadêmico** - Google Scholar in Portuguese
- **ABNT norms** - Brazilian citation and formatting standards

## Brazilian Academic Context

### ABNT Norms (Associação Brasileira de Normas Técnicas)
- **NBR 6023** - Bibliographic references
- **NBR 6024** - Section numbering
- **NBR 6027** - Summaries
- **NBR 6028** - Abstracts
- **NBR 14724** - Thesis and dissertation presentation
- Use ABNT styles in Word, LaTeX, or reference managers

### Brazilian Academic Culture
- **CAPES** - Coordination for Improvement of Higher Education Personnel (grades programs)
- **CNPq** - National Council for Scientific and Technological Development (funding)
- **FAPs** - State research funding agencies (FAPERGS, FAPESP, etc.)
- **Strictu sensu** - Master's and Doctorate programs
- **Lato sensu** - Specialization and MBA programs
- **Defesa de tese** - Thesis defense (public event)

### Academic Writing in Portuguese
- Formal register (você, third person)
- Avoid colloquialisms
- Use academic vocabulary
- Clear sentence structure
- Cohesion and coherence
- Citation integration (according to ABNT)
