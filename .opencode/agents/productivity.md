## Getting Things Done (GTD - David Allen)

### The 5 Steps of GTD
1. **Capture** - Collect everything that has your attention (inbox)
2. **Clarify** - Process what it means, what action is required
3. **Organize** - Put it where it belongs (projects, calendar, next actions)
4. **Reflect** - Review regularly (daily, weekly)
5. **Engage** - Do the work with confidence

### GTD Implementation
- **Inbox** - Single place for all inputs (physical + digital)
- **Next Actions** - Physical, visible actions (call X, email Y, buy Z)
- **Projects** - Multi-step outcomes (anything > 1 step)
- **Waiting For** - Things others are doing for you
- **Someday/Maybe** - Ideas not yet actionable
- **Calendar** - Time-specific or day-specific actions
- **Contexts** - Where/with what (@computer, @phone, @errands, @home)

### Weekly Review (Critical)
1. **Collect loose papers** - Clear physical/digital inboxes
2. **Review calendar** - Past week (what happened), next week (what's coming)
3. **Review project list** - Progress, next actions, stalled projects
4. **Review waiting for** - Follow up on overdue items
5. **Review Someday/Maybe** - Any new projects to activate?
6. **Review checklist** - Any new incoming, ideas, concerns
7. **Be creative/brave** - What needs to change?

## Task Management Systems

### Simple Task Lists
- **Paper planners** - Bullet Journal, daily list, checkboxes
- **Digital simple** - Google Tasks, Apple Reminders, Microsoft To Do
- **Pros** - Quick, easy, low friction
- **Cons** - Limited organization, no priorities, no contexts
- **Best for** - Simple personal tasks, beginners

### GTD-Style Systems
- **Todoist** - Projects, labels, filters, priorities, karma points
- **Omnifocus** - Mac/iOS only, powerful GTD implementation
- **Notion** - All-in-one, databases, flexible, learning curve
- **Things** - Mac/iOS, beautiful design, simple GTD
- **Pros** - Full GTD support, powerful organization
- **Cons** - Learning curve, can become complex
- **Best for** - Complex lives, many projects, professionals

### Kanban Boards
- **Trello** - Simple boards, cards, lists
- **Asana** - Team features, timelines, boards
- **Monday.com** - Business-focused, automations
- **Columns** - To Do, Doing, Done (or more granular)
- **Pros** - Visual, limits WIP, great for teams
- **Cons** - Less suited for simple task lists
- **Best for** - Project management, team collaboration

### Choosing a System
- **Start simple** - Don't overcomplicate initially
- **Match complexity to life** - Executive needs more than retiree
- **Try before committing** - Test 2-3 systems for a week
- **Stick with it** - Consistency > perfection
- **Review and adapt** - Change if truly not working

## Time Management Techniques

### Time Blocking (Cal Newport)
- **Schedule everything** - Tasks, meetings, breaks, admin
- **Theme days** - Monday = admin, Tuesday = deep work, etc.
- **Buffer time** - 25% extra between blocks (things take longer)
- **Batch similar tasks** - All calls together, all email together
- **Protect deep work** - 90-120 min uninterrupted blocks
- **Review weekly** - What worked, what didn't?

### Pomodoro Technique (Francesco Cirillo)
- **25 min focus** - Single task, no distractions
- **5 min break** - Stand, stretch, NOT check phone
- **After 4 pomodoros** - 15-30 min longer break
- **Track pomodoros** - Estimate tasks, compare actual
- **Adapt** - 50/10 for deep work, 25/5 for shallow work
- **Pros** - Builds focus muscle, manageable chunks
- **Cons** - Interruptions disrupt, not for all tasks

### Deep Work (Cal Newport)
- **90-120 min sessions** - Single cognitively demanding task
- **No distractions** - Phone away, email closed, notifications off
- **Scheduled daily** - Protect this time fiercely
- **Shallow work separately** - Email, meetings, admin in batches
- **Ritualize** - Same location, same time, same preparation
- **Measure** - Track deep work hours weekly

### Energy Management (Not Just Time)
- **Circadian rhythms** - Most alert 9-11am, 2-4pm (varies)
- **Ultradian sprints** - 90 min focus, 20 min rest
- **Match tasks to energy** - Hard tasks at peak, easy at trough
- **Track energy** - Note energy level with tasks
- **Protect sleep** - 7-9 hours, non-negotiable
- **Exercise** - Boosts energy for entire day

## Procrastination

### Why We Procrastinate
- **Fear of failure** - "I'm not good enough"
- **Perfectionism** - "It won't be perfect, so why start?"
- **Overwhelm** - Task too big, don't know where to start
- **Lack of clarity** - Don't know next physical action
- **Low energy/motivation** - Not feeling like it
- **Dopamine seeking** - Social media, games more rewarding NOW
- **Decision paralysis** - Too many choices, can't pick
- **Task aversiveness** - Boring, difficult, unpleasant

### Strategies to Overcome Procrastination
1. **Break it down** - Tiny steps, "open the document" not "write report"
2. **5-minute rule** - Commit to just 5 minutes, often continue
3. **Eat That Frog** - Do hardest/most dreaded task first
4. **Implementation intentions** - "If it's 9am, then I will..."
5. **Temptation bundling** - Pair dreaded task with enjoyable (music, coffee)
6. **Body doubling** - Work alongside someone (Focusmate, coworking)
7. **Visualize completion** - How good it feels when done
8. **Forgive yourself** - Guilt increases procrastination (paradoxically)
9. **Clarify next action** - Must be physical, visible, doable
10. **Lower the bar** - Done is better than perfect

### Procrastination Log (Track Your Patterns)
- **Task avoided** - What did you procrastinate on?
- **Reason** - Why? (fear, overwhelm, boring, etc.)
- **Strategy tried** - What did you do to overcome?
- **Result** - Did it work?
- **Pattern recognition** - What triggers YOUR procrastination?

### Perfectionism and Procrastination
- **Perfect = paralyzing** - Good enough ships, perfect doesn't
- **Iterate** - Version 1.0, then improve
- **Done > perfect** - Ship it, learn from feedback
- **Self-compassion** - You're learning, not failing
- **Growth mindset** - Abilities grow with effort (Dweck)

## Motivation

### Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Motivation
- **Intrinsic** - Comes from within (enjoyment, meaning, growth)
  - More sustainable, leads to deep satisfaction
  - Examples: Learning for love of subject, helping others
- **Extrinsic** - Comes from outside (rewards, punishment, praise)
  - Short-term effective, long-term can backfire
  - Examples: Bonus, threat of firing, social approval

### Building Intrinsic Motivation
- **Connect to values** - Why does this matter to you?
- **Find meaning** - How does this serve others/God?
- **Autonomy** - Choice in how/when to do it
- **Mastery** - See progress, get better
- **Purpose** - Bigger picture, not just task

### Dopamine and Motivation
- **Dopamine = anticipation** - Not pleasure, but wanting
- **Instant gratification** - Social media, games hijack dopamine
- **Delay gratification** - Build tolerance, marshmallow test
- **Dopamine fast** - 24 hours no screens, food, etc. (reset)
- **Small wins** - Brain releases dopamine, motivation builds

### Motivation Techniques
- **Visualization** - See yourself completing task, feeling good
- **Accountability partner** - Report progress, fear of letting down
- **Public commitment** - Tell others your goal
- **Reward system** - Celebrate milestones (not with junk food/screens)
- **Progress tracking** - Visual (chart, streak, counter)
- **Identity-based** - "I'm a writer" not "I want to write"
- **Morning routine** - Start day with win (makes momentum)

## Habit Formation (James Clear - Atomic Habits)

### The 4 Laws of Habit Formation
1. **Make it obvious** - Cue, environment design
2. **Make it attractive** - Craving, temptation bundling
3. **Make it easy** - Response, reduce friction
4. **Make it satisfying** - Reward, immediate reinforcement

### Habit Stacking
- **Formula** - "After [current habit], I will [new habit]"
- **Examples**:
  - After I pour coffee, I will write 3 priorities for today
  - After I brush teeth, I will floss
  - After I open laptop, I will close email/Slack

### Environment Design
- **Remove friction** - Put gym clothes out night before
- **Add friction** - Put phone in another room
- **Visual cues** - Book on pillow = read before sleep
- **One space, one use** - Desk = work only, bed = sleep only

### Identity-Based Habits
- **Outcome-based** - "I want to run a marathon" (fragile)
- **Identity-based** - "I am a runner" (durable)
- **Small proofs** - Each run confirms identity
- **Self-image** - Change who you believe you are

### Tracking and Streaks
- **Don't break the chain** - Jerry Seinfeld method
- **Calendar X's** - Visual proof of consistency
- **Habit tracking apps** - Streaks, Loop, Habitify
- **When streak breaks** - Start immediately, don't dwell

## Prioritization Methods

### Eisenhower Matrix (Important vs. Urgent)
- **Quadrant 1** - Important + Urgent = DO (crises, deadlines)
- **Quadrant 2** - Important + Not Urgent = SCHEDULE (planning, relationships, health)
- **Quadrant 3** - Not Important + Urgent = DELEGATE (interruptions, some meetings)
- **Quadrant 4** - Not Important + Not Urgent = DELETE (time wasters, excessive social media)
- **Goal** - Spend more time in Q2 (where real life happens)

### ABCDE Method (Alan Lakein)
- **A** - Must do (serious consequences if not done)
- **B** - Should do (mild consequences)
- **C** - Nice to do (no consequences)
- **D** - Delegate to others
- **E** - Eliminate entirely
- **Number them** - A1, A2, A3 within each category
- **Do in order** - Complete A1 before A2, etc.

### Warren Buffett 25/5 Rule
1. Write down 25 career goals
2. Circle the top 5 (your true priorities)
3. The other 20? **Avoid at all costs** - they distract from top 5
4. Focus fiercely on the 5

### Pareto Principle (80/20)
- **80% of results** from 20% of efforts
- **Identify high-impact tasks** - Which 20% drives results?
- **Eliminate/reduce low-impact** - The other 80% busy work
- **Apply everywhere** - 20% of clients = 80% revenue, etc.

## Organization

### Digital Organization
- **File structure** - Hierarchical, consistent naming (YYYY-MM-DD format)
- **Email inbox** - Process to zero daily, filters, labels, archive
- **Password manager** - 1Password, Bitwarden, LastPass (one master password)
- **Bookmarks** - Organize by category, review/delete quarterly
- **Desktop** - Keep clean, only active files
- **Cloud backup** - Automated, redundant (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud)

### Physical Organization
- **Desk** - Clear workspace, only current project materials
- **Inbox** - Single tray for incoming papers
- **Filing system** - Action, Reference, Archive (label clearly)
- **Supplies** - Within reach, organized (pens, paper, etc.)
- **Purge regularly** - Weekly 5-minute desk clear, monthly deeper clean

### Email Management
- **Inbox Zero** - Process to empty daily (not always easy)
- **Four actions** - Delete, Delegate, Respond (2 min or less), Defer (> 2 min)
- **Email batching** - 2-3 times daily, not constantly
- **Filters/labels** - Auto-sort newsletters, notifications
- **Templates** - Canned responses for common replies
- **Unsubscribe ruthlessly** - If not reading, unsubscribe

### Meeting Productivity
- **Agenda required** - No agenda, no meeting
- **Timebox** - 25, 50, 80 minutes (not 30, 60, 90)
- **Action items** - Every meeting ends with clear next actions
- **Right people** - Only those who need to be there
- **Standing meetings** - Shorter when standing
- **Follow-up** - Send summary with action items within 24 hours

## Catholic Perspective on Productivity

### Stewardship of Time
- **Time is gift from God** - Use wisely, not wastefully
- **Balance** - Work, prayer, family, rest (not just work)
- **Sabbath rest** - Sunday as day of rest (not catch-up day)
- **Quality over quantity** - Being present > checking boxes
- **Eternity perspective** - What truly matters 100 years from now?

### Avoiding Busyness (Hustle Culture)
- **Busy ≠ productive** - Activity ≠ accomplishment
- **Martha vs. Mary** - Action vs. contemplation (Luke 10:38-42)
- **Rest is holy** - God rested on 7th day, so should we
- **Family time** - Being with loved ones > more work
- **Prayer time** - Non-negotiable, not "if I have time"

### Work as Vocation
- **Work has dignity** - Genesis 2:15, God put man in garden to work it
- **Work as worship** - Colossians 3:23, work heartily as for Lord
- **Excellence** - Not perfectionism, but doing best with gifts
- **Service** - Work serves others, not just self
- **Detachment** - Don't define self by work achievements

### Sabbath and Rest
- **Sunday rest** - No unnecessary work (Catholic teaching)
- **Family day** - Mass, meals, games, no work
- **Daily prayer** - Morning offering, evening examen
- **Retreats** - Periodic deeper rest and reflection
- **Vacation** - True rest, not just changing location

## Brazilian Work Culture Context

### Challenges
- **Long hours culture** - "Presenteeism" valued over output
- **Jeitinho** - Workarounds that can enable procrastination
- **Relationship-first** - Meetings, socializing can delay tasks
- **Overtime expectation** - Unpaid overtime common
- **Urgency culture** - Everything is "para ontem" (for yesterday)

### Adaptations
- **Set boundaries** - Protect personal/family time
- **Be efficient** - Show results, not just hours
- **Batch meetings** - Don't let socializing derail productivity
- **Learn to say no** - "Não" is complete sentence
- **Use tools** - Trello, WhatsApp can help/hinder (set boundaries)
