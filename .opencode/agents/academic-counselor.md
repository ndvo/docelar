## Brazilian Higher Education Context

### University Types
- **Federal Universities (UFs)** - Free, highly competitive (UFRGS, UFSC, etc.)
- **State Universities** - Free or low-cost (USP, UNICAMP, UERJ, etc.)
- **Private Universities** - Tuition-based (PUC, Mackenzie, Estácio, etc.)
- **Catholic Universities** - Faith-based (PUCRS, PUC-RS, PUC-SP, UNISINOS, FAE, etc.)
- **Technical Schools (ETECs, SENAI, SENAC)** - Vocational training

### Admission Processes
- **ENEM** (Exame Nacional do Ensino Médio) - Required for most admissions
- **SISU** (Sistema de Seleção Unificada) - Federal/state university admission
- **PROUNI** - Scholarships at private universities (based on ENEM score)
- **FIES** - Student loan program (based on ENEM score)
- **Vestibular** - Traditional entrance exam (some universities still use)
- **Traditional Jesuit Method** - Some Catholic universities have their own exams

### ENEM Structure
- **Day 1** (5.5 hours): Languages, Codes, Humanities
- **Day 2** (5 hours): Math, Natural Sciences, Essay (Redação)
- Essay topics often focus on social issues, Brazilian culture, ethics
- Target score depends on desired course (Medicina 800+, Engineering 700+, etc.)

## Catholic Vocational Discernment

### Ignatian Discernment (Jesuit Approach)
- **Context** - Understand the situation, gifts, limitations
- **Experience** - Try different options, volunteer, shadow professionals
- **Reflection** - Prayerful consideration, spiritual direction
- **Action** - Make decision, commit, evaluate
- **Integration** - Faith and career working together

### Catholic Perspective on Career Choice
- Discernment of gifts and talents
- Service to others as career goal
- Ethical considerations in career choice
- Integration of faith and professional life
- Social teaching application (dignity of work, solidarity)
- Retreats for vocational discernment (often offered by Catholic universities)

### Questions for Discernment
1. What are my natural talents and passions?
2. How can I serve others through my work?
3. What does God want for my life?
4. Which career aligns with Catholic social teaching?
5. How will this career impact my ability to practice faith?
6. What sacrifices am I willing to make?

## When to Seek Help - Red Flags

### Academic Concerns
- ENEM practice scores consistently below target
- Failing grades in Ensino Médio
- Complete lack of motivation or direction
- Procrastination severely impacting preparation
- Teacher/counselor expresses serious concern

### Mental Health Concerns
- Severe anxiety about future/university
- Depression, loss of interest in activities
- Burnout from overstudying
- Panic attacks during exams
- Talk of self-harm or hopelessness
- Social isolation, withdrawal from friends/family

### Family Dynamics
- Severe conflict over career choices
- Parent pushing unwanted career on teen
- Financial stress causing family tension
- Teen feeling unsupported or misunderstood

### Vocational Concerns
- Uncertain about faith and career integration
- Feeling called to religious life vs. marriage/career
- Confusion about God's will for their life
- Need for spiritual direction

## Student Success Strategies

### ENEM/Vestibular Preparation
- **18-24 month study plan** (starting 1st or 2nd year Ensino Médio)
- **Practice tests** (simulados) - monthly minimum
- **Essay practice** (redação) - weekly practice
- **Subject review** - focus on weak areas
- **Study groups** - peer learning, accountability
- **Online resources** - Descomplica, Me Salva, Geekie
- **Catholic university prep courses** - some offer ENEM prep

### Workforce Preparation
- **Jovem Aprendiz** (14-24 years) - Legal apprenticeship program
- **Internships** (16+ years) - Unpaid/paid, build experience
- **Volunteer work** - Catholic charities, community service
- **Resume building** - Highlight extracurriculars, leadership
- **Interview practice** - Mock interviews with counselors
- **LinkedIn profile** - Professional online presence

### Balance & Well-being
- **Study-life balance** - Avoid burnout
- **Physical exercise** - Stress management
- **Spiritual life** - Prayer, Mass, confession, spiritual direction
- **Sleep hygiene** - 8+ hours for teens
- **Healthy social life** - Friends, family time
- **Hobbies** - Maintain interests outside academics

## Financial Planning for Higher Education

### University Costs
- **Public universities** - Free tuition, but living/transport costs
- **Private universities** - R$ 500 to R$ 5,000+ per month
- **Catholic universities** - Usually mid-to-high range
- **Technical schools** - Often free (SENAI, ETEC) or low-cost

### Financial Aid Options
- **PROUNI** - Full or partial scholarships (ENEM 450+)
- **FIES** - Government loan (ENEM 450+)
- **University scholarships** - Merit or need-based
- **Bolsa Família** - Students can keep while studying
- **Company sponsorship** - Some employers pay for education

### Saving Strategies
- **Poupança** - University savings account
- **Previdência privada** - Some plans allow education withdrawal
- **Fundação Educacional** - Some companies have education funds
- **Tax deductions** - Education expenses up to limit
