## Brazilian Education Context

### Education Levels
- **Educação Infantil** (0-5 years) - creche and pré-escola
- **Ensino Fundamental I** (6-10 years, 1º ao 5º ano)
- **Ensino Fundamental II** (11-14 years, 6º ao 9º ano)
- **Ensino Médio** (15-17 years, 1º ao 3º ano)
- **Higher Education** - university (public: federal/estadual, private)

### BNCC (Base Nacional Comum Curricular)
- General competencies (10 competencies)
- Specific competencies by area (Languages, Mathematics, Sciences, etc.)
- Early childhood education goals
- Catholic schools follow BNCC + religious education component

### Catholic School Culture
- Daily prayer/mass participation
- Religion classes (Ensino Religioso)
- Sacrament preparation (First Communion, Confirmation) often at school
- Retreats (retiros) for older students
- Community service requirements
- Values-based discipline (virtues, character education)
- Feast day celebrations (Santo Antônio, Nossa Senhora, etc.)

### Parent-Teacher Dynamics
- **Reunião de pais e mestres** - typically 2-4 times per year
- **Boletim** - quarterly report card (trimestral/bimestral)
- **WhatsApp groups** - common communication channel
- **Agenda escolar** - daily communication notebook
- **Conselho de classe** - when student is struggling academically
- **Pedagogical meetings** - when learning difficulties arise

## Pedagogic Theories & Application

### Piaget - Cognitive Development
- Sensorimotor (0-2 years)
- Preoperational (2-7 years)
- Concrete operational (7-11 years)
- Formal operational (11+ years)
- Apply: Age-appropriate learning activities and expectations

### Vygotsky - Sociocultural Theory
- Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)
- Scaffolding techniques
- Social learning importance
- Apply: Parent support strategies, peer learning

### Montessori
- Independence and self-directed learning
- Prepared environment
- Sensitive periods
- Apply: Home learning environment setup, Catholic Montessori options

### Ignatian Pedagogy (Jesuit)
- Context - understand the learner's situation
- Experience - engage with content
- Reflection - think critically
- Action - apply learning
- Evaluation - assess growth
- Apply: Catholic school approach, spiritual integration

### Paulo Freire (Brazilian Educator)
- Critical pedagogy
- Dialogue-based learning
- Conscientization (critical consciousness)
- Apply: Discussion at home, social justice education

### Active Learning Methodologies
- Project-based learning (PBL)
- Problem-based learning
- Flipped classroom
- Gamification
- Apply: Study techniques, homework support

## When to Seek Help - Academic Red Flags

### Academic Concerns
- Failing grades in 2+ subjects
- Significant drop in performance (> 1 letter grade)
- Cannot read at grade level by 3rd year Fundamental
- Math skills 2+ years behind
- Constant homework struggles despite effort
- Teacher expresses concern about learning

### Social/Emotional Concerns
- Bullying (victim or perpetrator)
- Social isolation, no friends
- School refusal, frequent complaints of illness
- Extreme anxiety about tests or presentations
- Behavioral issues at school
- Loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities

### When to Consider Evaluation
- Suspected learning disabilities (dyslexia, dyscalculia, ADHD)
- Giftedness evaluation (altas habilidades/superdotação)
- Speech/language delays
- Sensory processing issues
- Autism spectrum evaluation

## Homeschooling in Brazil

### Current Legal Status (2026)
- Not fully legalized - requires judicial authorization
- Some families pursue "educação domiciliar" through courts
- Must follow BNCC curriculum
- Periodic evaluations required
- Growing movement, but still restricted

### If Considering Homeschooling
- Research current legislation
- Connect with Catholic homeschool groups
- Plan curriculum aligned with BNCC
- Prepare for social activities (sports, groups)
- Document learning rigorously
- Consider hybrid models (some subjects at home)

## School Choice Considerations

### Public Schools (Escola Pública)
- Federal, estadual, municipal options
- Free, but variable quality
- Usually neighborhood-based
- Less religious formation

### Private Schools (Escola Particular)
- Monthly tuition (mensalidade)
- Variable quality and focus
- More parent involvement expected
- May offer bilingual programs

### Catholic Schools (Escola Católica)
- Religious formation integrated
- Values-based education
- Sacrament preparation on-site
- Often higher academic standards
- Monthly tuition (usually less than elite private)
- Strong parent community

### Financial Support
- PROUNI (university scholarship)
- FIES (student loan)
- School vouchers (bolsas de estudo) - private schools
- Tax deductions for education expenses
